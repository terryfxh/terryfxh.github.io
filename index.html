01

Cointegration & Mean-Reversion: An Out-of-Sample Reality Check

Tested whether a textbook cointegration and Ornstein–Uhlenbeck pairs strategy could survive out of sample on real ETF data (EWA/EWC). I estimated the hedge ratio with Engle–Granger (ADF) and fixed the OU parameters in sample. It did not survive: the out-of-sample Sharpe was −0.11 and remained negative at every entry threshold tested. The cause was an 83-day half-life that permitted only three trades, together with marginal cointegration and a regime change. A disciplined test of the kind that catches overfitting before any capital is at risk.

02

Monte Carlo Option Pricer: Variance Reduction

Priced European and arithmetic-Asian options by risk-neutral Monte Carlo and validated the results against closed-form Black–Scholes, agreeing to within roughly 1.5 standard errors. I implemented antithetic and control-variate estimators, achieving a 6.8× variance reduction on the European call and about 36× on the Asian via a geometric-average control, and verified the 1/√N convergence rate over 40 runs.

03

Volatility Forecasting: GARCH vs. Machine Learning

Implemented GARCH(1,1) from scratch by maximum likelihood and benchmarked it against an EWMA baseline and gradient-boosted trees for one-day-ahead volatility, scored out of sample with proxy-consistent losses (MSE and QLIKE). On a known GARCH process the estimator recovered the true parameters and the parametric model clearly beat the machine-learning model. A deliberately contrarian result, with the further lesson that a noisy r² proxy can barely separate models that differ substantially against the truth.
