01

Cointegration & Mean-Reversion — an Out-of-Sample Reality Check

Tested whether a textbook cointegration + Ornstein–Uhlenbeck pairs strategy survives out-of-sample on real ETF data (EWA/EWC). Estimated the hedge ratio via Engle–Granger (ADF) and froze the OU parameters in-sample. Verdict: it does not — out-of-sample Sharpe −0.11 and negative across every entry threshold. Diagnosed the cause (an 83-day half-life → only 3 trades, marginal cointegration, regime change). A disciplined test that catches overfitting before risking capital.

02

Monte Carlo Option Pricer — Variance Reduction

Priced European and arithmetic-Asian options by risk-neutral Monte Carlo, validated against closed-form Black–Scholes (within ~1.5 standard errors). Implemented antithetic and control-variate estimators (6.8× variance reduction on the European call; ~36× on the Asian via a geometric-average control), and verified the 1/√N convergence rate over 40 runs.

03

Volatility Forecasting — GARCH vs. Machine Learning

Implemented GARCH(1,1) from scratch by maximum likelihood and benchmarked it against an EWMA baseline and gradient-boosted trees for one-day-ahead volatility, scored out-of-sample with proxy-consistent losses (MSE, QLIKE). On a known GARCH process the MLE recovers the true parameters and the parametric model clearly beats ML — a deliberately contrarian result, plus the lesson that a noisy r² proxy can barely separate models that differ hugely against the truth.
